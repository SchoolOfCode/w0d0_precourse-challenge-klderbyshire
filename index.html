TOP 5 THINGS TO DO IN SYDNEY

Sydney Harbour By Night

This cosmopolitan hub of Australia is filled with endless beaches, a thriving dining scene and fabulous ocean walks, just to name a few! This beautiful harbour city has a lot to offer for backpackers to luxury travellers so get your bucket lists ready. Cocktails on a roof top city bar? Surfing on Bondi? How about a breathtaking hike in the Blue Mountains? This adventurous city has it all and here is a little taste of whats on offer in this beautiful part of the world. Here are my top five things to do in Sydney...just to get you started.

VISIT THE ROYAL BOTANIC GARDENS

The Royal Botanic Gardens is the oldest Botanic Gardens in Australia. Being only a few minutes walk from the CBD and The Opera House makes it the perfect place to relax and enjoy half a day of sightseeing. As well as getting your steps in for the day, you will learn many interesting facts about the plant world and how they survive.

BONDI TO COOGEE WALK

This 6km walk will take around two to three hours depending on how often you stop. You can make this into a full day activity by taking a dip in many of the outdoor pools that you pass enroute. Spectacular ocean views, white beaches and rock pools await and is a must-do activity when in Sydney. You may even spot the resident whales and dolphins at the right time of year!

VISIT THE ROCKS

Rich in history and the first site of European settlement in Australia, The Rocks is well worth a visit. Well known for its cobblestone laneways, markets and dining scene, this area also offers fantastic views of the Sydney Harbour Bridge.

TAKE A CRUISE OR FERRY AROUND CIRCULAR QUAY OR DARLING HARBOUR

The ferry is a magnificent form of public transport and allows you time to relax and take in the stunning views around the multiple Bay areas. Take trips multiple parts of the city, including Manly, Neutral Bay and Rose Bay

VISIT THE FABULOUS SEASIDE TOWN OF MANLY

If your after relaxed seaside vibes, then Manly is your place to kick back and enjoy a sundowner with a cocktail in hand. Being a short (amazing) ferry ride from the Sydney CBD makes it the perfect choice for locals and toursits alike.Manly also boasts excellent al fresco dining options, a lovely museum, a sea life sanctuary, and even a shark-diving experience!
